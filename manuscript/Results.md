## Results

### Recovery of Near-Complete Adenovirus Genomes from Animal Metagenomic Samples

Metagenomic sequencing data from goat and dog samples were screened against an adenovirus reference panel. Mapping and consensus reconstruction identified two samples containing substantial adenovirus genomic signal.

Sample AIAMA_GOT005_OS_S43 generated a near-complete adenovirus genome most closely related to Human adenovirus 12 (AdV-12), while sample AINWZDOG001_S37 generated a near-complete genome most closely related to Human adenovirus 18 (AdV-18).

Consensus genome reconstruction produced genomes with high breadth of coverage across the corresponding reference genomes, supporting recovery of biologically meaningful viral genomes rather than isolated fragments or spurious alignments.

---

### Genome Coverage Profiles

Coverage profiling demonstrated broad read support across both reconstructed genomes.

The goat-derived AdV-12-like genome exhibited coverage distributed across the full adenovirus genome, with only limited low-coverage regions. Similarly, the dog-derived AdV-18-like genome showed consistent coverage across most genomic regions.

The broad distribution of mapped reads indicates that genome recovery was supported by sequencing evidence spanning the viral genome rather than being driven by a small number of highly conserved loci.

These coverage profiles provide confidence in the consensus sequences used for downstream phylogenetic and comparative analyses.

---

### BLAST Characterization of Recovered Genomes

BLAST analysis against the NCBI nucleotide database confirmed that both reconstructed genomes belong to established human adenovirus lineages.

The goat-derived genome showed highest similarity to Human adenovirus 12 genomes, including complete genomes from North America and an African sequence from Kenya. The strongest matches displayed nucleotide identities approaching 99% with genome-scale alignments covering essentially the entire recovered genome.

The dog-derived genome showed highest similarity to Human adenovirus 18 genomes, including complete genomes from Tanzania and reference AdV-18 genomes available in GenBank. Genome-scale alignments again demonstrated high nucleotide identity and extensive query coverage.

Together, these findings indicate that the recovered genomes are best classified as AdV-12-like and AdV-18-like adenoviruses respectively.

---

### Phylogenetic Placement of Recovered Adenovirus Genomes

To investigate evolutionary relationships, maximum-likelihood phylogenetic trees were constructed using the recovered genomes, reference adenovirus genomes, closest BLAST matches, Nigerian clinical adenovirus sequences, and non-human mastadenovirus outgroups.

The goat-derived consensus genome clustered within the Human mastadenovirus A lineage and grouped with established AdV-12 genomes. The genome formed a clade containing global AdV-12 isolates together with an African contextual sequence from Kenya, supporting classification as an AdV-12-like genome.

The dog-derived consensus genome clustered within the AdV-18 lineage and grouped closely with Tanzanian AdV-18 genomes and established AdV-18 references. This placement confirms that the recovered genome is evolutionarily consistent with known AdV-18 diversity.

In contrast, previously reported Nigerian clinical adenovirus genomes clustered separately within the enteric adenovirus lineage and did not group with either recovered genome.

The inclusion of canine and bovine adenoviruses as outgroups provided rooting context and confirmed that both recovered genomes fall within human-associated adenovirus diversity rather than non-human mastadenovirus lineages.

---

### Genome-Wide Variant Landscape

Comparison of each recovered genome against its closest reference sequence revealed substantial genomic variation distributed across the viral genomes.

The goat-derived AdV-12-like genome contained 487 detected variants, comprising 474 single nucleotide variants (SNVs) and 13 insertion/deletion events.

The dog-derived AdV-18-like genome contained 336 detected variants, comprising 331 SNVs and 5 insertion/deletion events.

Genome-wide variant landscape plots demonstrated that variation is distributed across the viral genomes rather than being restricted to a single genomic region. Multiple structural and capsid-associated genes contained elevated numbers of variants, indicating ongoing divergence from the reference genomes.

---

### Functional Annotation of Coding Variants

Coding-region annotation showed that most detected variants occurred within annotated adenovirus genes.

For the goat-derived genome, 424 variants occurred within coding regions, while only 63 variants occurred within non-coding regions. The largest numbers of coding variants were observed in the hexon protein, fiber protein, E2A DNA-binding protein, penton protein, and other structural genes.

For the dog-derived genome, 323 variants occurred within coding regions and 13 variants occurred within non-coding regions. High-variant genes included the hexon protein, protein IIIa precursor, fiber protein, DNA-binding protein, penton protein, and DNA polymerase.

Filtering to high-confidence coding SNVs showed that synonymous substitutions were more common than amino-acid-changing mutations in both genomes. The goat-derived genome contained 274 synonymous and 117 missense variants, while the dog-derived genome contained 162 synonymous and 53 missense variants.

The concentration of variation within major structural proteins such as hexon, fiber, and penton suggests that these regions contribute substantially to the observed divergence from available reference genomes.

Overall, the variant annotation results indicate that both recovered genomes represent naturally diverged adenovirus lineages while retaining the genomic architecture characteristic of Human mastadenovirus A.
